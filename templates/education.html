Public Education on Fire Incidents




Frequently Asked Questions


1. What should I do if there is a fire in my home?

If there is a fire in your home, evacuate immediately and call 911. Do not try to put out a large fire yourself.

2. How can I prevent a fire in my home?

To prevent fires, install smoke alarms, keep flammable items away from heat sources, and never leave cooking unattended.

3. What should I include in a fire escape plan?

A fire escape plan should include multiple exits from each room, a meeting point outside, and practice drills with your family.

4. How often should I test my smoke alarms?

Test your smoke alarms monthly and replace the batteries at least once a year. Replace the entire unit every 10 years.

5. What are the most common causes of house fires?

The most common causes of house fires include cooking accidents, heating equipment, electrical issues, and smoking.

6. How do I use a fire extinguisher?

Remember the acronym PASS: Pull the pin, Aim at the base of the fire, Squeeze the handle, and Sweep side to side.

7. What should I do if my clothes catch on fire?

If your clothes catch fire, stop where you are, drop to the ground, and roll over to smother the flames.

8. Are candles safe to use in my home?

Candles can be safe if used properly. Never leave a burning candle unattended, and keep them away from flammable items.

9. What is the role of fire sprinklers in fire safety?

Fire sprinklers can control or extinguish a fire before the fire department arrives, providing crucial time for escape.

10. How can I safely use a space heater?

Keep space heaters at least three feet away from anything flammable, never leave them on unattended, and follow the manufacturer’s instructions.




Additional Fire Safety Tips

  • Install smoke alarms on every level of your home, inside bedrooms, and outside sleeping areas.
  • Create and practice a home fire escape plan with all members of your household.
  • Keep a fire extinguisher in an easily accessible location, and make sure everyone knows how to use it.
  • Store flammable materials in a safe place, away from heat sources.
  • Never overload electrical outlets or use frayed extension cords.
  • Keep matches and lighters out of reach of children.
  • Regularly inspect and clean chimneys and heating equipment.
  • Use caution when cooking, and never leave cooking food unattended.
  • Ensure that your home’s address is clearly visible from the street for emergency responders.
  • Educate children about the dangers of fire and what to do in case of an emergency.
